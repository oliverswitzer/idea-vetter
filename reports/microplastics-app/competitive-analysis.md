# Competitive Landscape: Food Safety & Microplastics Scanner Apps

**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Category:** Food Safety / Ingredient Scanning / Microplastics Detection

---

## Executive Summary

The food safety scanner app market is mature and crowded, dominated by Yuka (80M users, $20M+ revenue) and Bobby Approved (137K ratings). A new wave of microplastics-focused apps has emerged since late 2024, but these are uniformly early-stage (most under 20 ratings), AI-estimation-based, and face serious credibility challenges. No app currently provides lab-verified microplastics data for food products. The gap between consumer anxiety about microplastics and the ability of any app to deliver reliable, actionable data is enormous. Bryan Johnson's Blueprint test kit ($150 blood test) validates willingness to pay among health-obsessed consumers, but the science of food-level microplastics detection remains unsettled. Portable hardware detectors are still in the lab prototype stage with no consumer product on the market.

## 2. Review Themes

### Top Complaints
1. **Inconsistent / contradictory ratings** -- identical products get wildly different scores
2. **Aggressive paywalls / subscription pressure**
3. **Missing products / incomplete databases**
4. **Oversimplified binary judgments**
5. **AI misidentification (microplastics apps)** -- fundamental credibility problem
6. **Methodological bias** -- opaque scoring

### Top Praise
1. **Awareness and education** -- the learning effect is the primary value
2. **Speed and convenience of barcode scanning**
3. **Healthier alternative suggestions**
4. **Independence from brands**

---

## 3. Gaps and Opportunities

1. **No Lab-Verified Microplastics Data** -- every app uses AI estimation; partner with labs for a real moat
2. **Total Contaminant View** -- no dominant app covers additives + microplastics + PFAS + heavy metals + pesticides
3. **Actionable Guidance Beyond Scores** -- personalized reduction plans, substitution guides
4. **Restaurant and Unpackaged Food** -- all apps require barcodes
5. **Water-Specific Analysis** -- high exposure vector, no dedicated app
6. **Trust and Methodology Transparency** -- #1 complaint across all apps

---

## 4. Key Signal

**Olive** is the one to watch. It is the only Tier 2 app explicitly covering microplastics alongside other contaminants with meaningful traction. If microplastics scanning matters, Olive will likely absorb demand before a pure-play microplastics app can scale.
