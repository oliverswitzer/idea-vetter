Break down the following idea into structured, researchable components: $ARGUMENTS

## Step 1: Restate the Idea
Write a single clear sentence describing what the product does, for whom, and why.

## Step 2: Identify Core Components
- **Target user**: Who has this problem? Be specific (role, company size, situation).
- **Job to be done**: What task or outcome are they trying to achieve?
- **Trigger moment**: What event makes them search for a solution?
- **Expected outcome**: What does success look like for the user?

## Step 3: Generate Market Angles
List 3-5 distinct ways this idea could be positioned:
- Different user segments
- Different use cases
- Different pricing models
- Different distribution channels

## Step 4: List Assumptions
Enumerate the assumptions that must be true for this idea to work. Categorize as:
- **Problem assumptions**: The problem exists and is painful enough
- **Market assumptions**: The market is big enough and reachable
- **Solution assumptions**: This approach solves it better than alternatives
- **Business assumptions**: People will pay and the unit economics work

## Step 5: Generate Research Questions
For each assumption, write 1-2 specific questions that research should answer. These become the agenda for evidence collection.
